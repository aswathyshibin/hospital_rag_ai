HOSPITAL PATIENT ADMISSION STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)

1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this SOP is to define the standardized process for admitting patients into the hospital to ensure safety, accuracy, and efficiency.

2. SCOPE
This procedure applies to all hospital staff including receptionists, nurses, doctors, and administrative personnel involved in patient admission.

3. RESPONSIBILITIES
- Front Desk Staff: Collect patient details and verify documents
- Nursing Staff: Initial assessment and vitals
- Doctors: Clinical evaluation
- Administration: Bed allocation and billing initiation

4. ADMISSION TYPES
4.1 Emergency Admission
Emergency patients must be admitted immediately without delay. Identification and documentation may be completed after stabilization.

4.2 Scheduled Admission
Patients with planned procedures must report to the admission desk at the scheduled time with valid identification and referral documents.

5. REQUIRED DOCUMENTS
- Government-issued ID proof
- Insurance card (if applicable)
- Referral letter
- Previous medical records

6. ADMISSION PROCESS
Step 1: Patient arrives at admission desk
Step 2: Registration form is completed
Step 3: Unique hospital ID is generated
Step 4: Nursing staff records vital signs
Step 5: Doctor performs initial examination
Step 6: Bed is allocated

7. SPECIAL CASES
- Pediatric patients require guardian consent
- Unconscious patients follow emergency protocol

8. RECORD MAINTENANCE
All admission records must be stored securely in the hospital information system.

9. CONFIDENTIALITY
Patient information must be handled according to hospital privacy policies.

10. REVISION HISTORY
This SOP shall be reviewed annually.
