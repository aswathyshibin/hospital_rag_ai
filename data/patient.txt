HOSPITAL PATIENT ADMISSION STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE (SOP)

1. PURPOSE
The purpose of this SOP is to define the standardized process for admitting patients into the hospital.
This ensures patient safety, accurate documentation, and efficient coordination among departments.

2. SCOPE
This procedure applies to all hospital staff involved in patient admission, including:
- Reception staff
- Nursing staff
- Doctors
- Administrative staff

3. TYPES OF ADMISSION
3.1 Emergency Admission
Emergency admission is initiated when a patient requires immediate medical attention.
The patient is taken directly to the emergency department for assessment and treatment.

3.2 Elective Admission
Elective admission is planned in advance based on a doctor’s recommendation.
Patients are admitted at a scheduled date and time.

4. EMERGENCY ADMISSION PROCEDURE
1. The patient is received at the emergency department.
2. The triage nurse assesses the patient’s condition immediately.
3. Emergency medical care is provided without delay.
4. Basic patient details are recorded once the patient is stabilized.
5. The duty doctor decides on further treatment or admission.

5. ROLES AND RESPONSIBILITIES
5.1 Reception Staff
- Register patient details
- Create admission records
- Guide patient attendants

5.2 Nursing Staff
- Perform triage assessment
- Monitor vital signs
- Assist doctors during treatment

5.3 Doctors
- Diagnose the patient
- Decide admission type
- Prescribe treatment plan

6. DOCUMENTATION
The following documents must be maintained:
- Patient admission form
- Medical history record
- Consent forms
- Insurance details (if applicable)

7. CONFIDENTIALITY
All patient information must be handled confidentially according to hospital policy.
Unauthorized access or disclosure is strictly prohibited.

8. REVIEW AND UPDATES
This SOP shall be reviewed annually and updated as required.
